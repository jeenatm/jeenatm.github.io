About

My journey into health research is rooted in personal experience. Growing up with asthma in Bangladesh, I witnessed the challenges of managing a chronic illness within an under-resourced healthcare system. These early experiences shaped my commitment to improving health outcomes.

With over seven years of academic and research experience in Economics, I transitioned into the field of Pharmaceutical Sciences. This interdisciplinary path has allowed me to explore complex questions at the intersection of economics, health services, and policy research with a strong focus on equity, knowledge translation, and real-world impact.

I am particularly passionate about clinical prediction models, economic evaluation, and health outcomes research that informs decision-making for clinicians, policymakers, and health systems. Always curious to learn more and open to meaningful conversations and collaborations. Please do not hesitate to connect!

Research Interests

  • Health economics & outcomes research
  • Clinical prediction modelling
  • Population-based administrative data analysis
  • Knowledge translation
